About Us

Preparation + Opportunity = Success

The Strengthening The Empowerment Process Foundation is a federally recognized 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization created in 2015, founded by three African-American police officers to improve young people's lives.

Who We Are

Founded to Make a Difference

STEP Foundation was founded by three African-American police officers who saw a need in the community and decided to act. Our mission is to provide opportunities for financially challenged children to achieve their aspirations and realize their dreams.

We believe that experience is a great teacher, community is a great classroom, and life is a great school. STEP knows that nothing stays with a young person like the memories that come from applying lessons in real world situations and being involved in life changing circumstances that reveal positive results.

Our vision is to create quality, sustainable youth programs in underserved communities across Los Angeles, giving every young person the tools and mentorship they need to succeed.

Our Core Values

What Guides Us

Experience as Teacher

Real-world experiences shape young minds. We create opportunities for youth to learn through hands-on involvement and meaningful challenges.

Community as Classroom

The neighborhood is where lessons come alive. We use community settings to teach teamwork, leadership, and civic responsibility.

Life as School

Every moment is a chance to grow. We prepare youth for the real world with skills that extend far beyond the basketball court.

College "attendance" is not a goal — college "graduation" is an expectation.

Our Leadership

Meet the Founders

Three dedicated leaders who turned their vision of community empowerment into action.

TF

President

Travis Fenderson, M.A.

"People do more when someone else believes they can do more."

A husband, father, teacher, coach, and role model. Travis brings experience from high school and collegiate basketball, youth and community organizations, teaching, and a career in law enforcement.

GM

Vice President

Geoffrey Meeks

"The future is dependent upon the development of each community's future leaders."

A Los Angeles native, public school graduate, and college basketball scholarship recipient. Geoffrey is a husband, father, mentor, and leader with a career in law enforcement.

JJ

Secretary

Jelani Janisse

"The mentor-mentee relationship is a two way street — both lives develop one another."

Known as Coach "J," Jelani is a University of Kansas graduate, former youth counselor, father, husband, entrepreneur, and life coach specializing in personal development.
