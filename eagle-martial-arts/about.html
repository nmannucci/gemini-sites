Our Story

More Than A Sport. A Way Of Growing Up.

Eagle Martial Arts opened its La Jolla doors in 2008 as a family-owned Taekwondo school with a simple idea: martial arts should leave you better than it found you — more disciplined, more respectful, more confident, and more focused. Not just on the mat, but at home and at school too.

Every class is structured, skillfully designed, and intentionally warm. We welcome beginners, shy kids, restless kids, kids with ADHD or motor skill challenges, teens trying to find themselves, and adults trying to reclaim their strength. Our job is to meet each student where they are and build them up from there.

We keep our facility fully insured and our equipment top-of-the-line because the small things matter. And because we believe the family that kicks together stays together, we offer tailor-made programs for entire families — parents, kids, and siblings all training under one roof.

Meet Our Master

Master Sam Hadaf

Master Sam is a 6th-degree certified black belt from the World Taekwondo Federation and has been teaching martial arts continuously since 1983 — more than forty years on the mat. Over that career, he has trained more than 300 black belt students across the world.

"Our goal is to instill modesty, perseverance, courtesy, and indomitable spirit — and to make every class enjoyable, yet constructive."

His classes are known for being well-planned and delivered with humor, so students stay engaged without losing the discipline that makes Taekwondo work. Martial arts is a family craft for the Hadafs — all three of Master Sam's sons are black belt holders.

Our Philosophy

The Four Tenets We Train By.

Master Sam teaches the traditional tenets of Taekwondo not as slogans, but as daily practice. Every class, every drill, every belt test comes back to these four words.

Tenet 01

Modesty

A black belt stays a beginner. Students learn that confidence is quiet and that respect starts with self-awareness.

Tenet 02

Perseverance

Hard things stay hard until you keep showing up. We celebrate the small milestones that add up to a black belt.

Tenet 03

Courtesy

Bow in. Bow out. Say thank you. Hold the door. Courtesy on the mat becomes courtesy at the dinner table.

Tenet 04

Indomitable Spirit

The goal isn't to never fall. It's to stand up one more time than you fell. That spirit is what we're really teaching.
